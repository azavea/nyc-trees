Collecting data on street trees leads to a greener and greater NYC!

Our street trees make our city cooler, our air cleaner, and our neighborhoods more beautiful. TreesCount! is New York City’s third census of our urban forest, and tree-loving citizens like you are helping us collect data.

New volunteer training and data collection has now ended for 2015. Expert staff and independent mappers trained in winter data collection are continuing to map, and all trained voluntreers will be able to help us finish mapping in 2016.

Once the census is complete, NYC Parks will launch a new Street Tree Map using TreesCount! data. You will be able to see and explore the forest, and record tree stewardship like weeding, watering and mulching.

There are also opportunities year round to get involved in caring for the urban forest. Check out NYC Parks stewardship page to find out more!
