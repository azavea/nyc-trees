What is TreesCount! 2015?

TreesCount! is NYC Parks’ initiative to map and assess every street tree on every block in New York City.

How do I become a voluntreer?

New voluntreer training and data collection has ended, but there are opportunities year round to get involved in caring for the urban forest. Check out NYC Parks stewardship page to find out more.

Where can I volunteer to map street trees?

Data collection in many neighborhoods was completed in 2015. If you’re a trained mapper, check out our progress map to find out which neighborhoods are complete, and learn where we still need your help!.

Voluntreer data collection will begin again in spring of 2016. If you are an experienced independent mapper, and are interested in voluntreering over the winter, please email Treescount@parks.nyc.gov to find out how.

If you missed voluntreer training for TreesCount! don’t worry – there are still other opportunities to get involved! Check out NYC Parks stewardship page to find out how you can help care for our urban forest year-round.

When will TreesCount! end?

As with our past censuses in 1995-96 and 2005-06, this data collection effort will take two seasons. Volunteers, community groups, and expert staff members are all vital contributors to this effort, and working together we can make sure every tree counts!

For additional information on NYC Parks, TreesCount! and past TreesCount! Censuses from 1995 and 2005 visit the NYC Parks website.
