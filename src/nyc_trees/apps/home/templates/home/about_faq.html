What is TreesCount! 2015?

TreesCount! is NYC Parks’ third decadal inventory to map and assess every street tree on every block in New York City.

How do I become a voluntreer?

TreesCount! 2015 has officially ended, but there are opportunities year round to get involved in caring for the urban forest. Check out NYC Parksâ€™ Stewardship page to find out more.

When can I view the data?

All the street tree data collected during TreesCount! will be given back to YOU in the new interactive NYC Street Tree Map that will allow you to view and learn about the trees in your neighborhood, and record and share stewardship activities. We invite you to explore the beta version of the map at {{ "https://tree-map.nycgovparks.org/"|urlize }}.

For additional information on NYC Parks, TreesCount! and past TreesCount! Censuses from 1995 and 2005 visit the NYC Parks website.
Further questions can be directed to {{ "TreesCount@parks.nyc.gov"|urlize }}.
